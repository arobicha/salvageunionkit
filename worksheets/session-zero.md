# Session Zero

A worksheet for the table to fill in together, or for a solo player to journal through alone. The Mediator (or solo player) records the answers here, then uses them to build out the world between sessions.

There are no right answers. There are only answers that make the wasteland feel like *yours*.

## How to Use This Worksheet

Each section gathers a different layer of the world. Work through them in order — earlier answers will shape later ones. Plan on roughly **60–90 minutes** for a full pass, or split across two short sessions.

Each question has a **Mediator note** underneath in italics. That's guidance for you, not the table — what to listen for, what makes a useful answer, what follow-ups might be worth asking. Skip them aloud; read them as you go.

If the table struggles with a question, **skip it**. Come back later, or leave it blank for a future session to fill in. Empty space is fuel for surprise.

## 1. The Crawler

*Before anywhere else: home. The Crawler is the table's vehicle, settlement, debt-trap, and shield. Ground it first — every other answer will hang off of it.*

### What is the Crawler's name, and what does the name mean?

> _

*Mediator note: Names carry the Crawler's history. "The Verdigris" hints at corrosion and patience; "Mama Coyote" hints at the founder. If the table picks something cool but blank, gently push: "Why that name? Who named it?"*

### What shape is it? How does it move?

> _

*Mediator note: Salvage Union lists quadrupedal titans, sleek tunnellers, bipedal walking cities. Push for something specific and visual. Is there one defining feature people see from a mile out?*

### What was it, before it was a Crawler?

> _

*Mediator note: Per the setting, Crawlers are jury-rigged from salvage. A corpo mining rig? A war machine left over from some forgotten battle? A civilian hauler that grew? The "before" seeds rumors, enemies, and structural quirks.*

### Who founded it, and are they still around?

> _

*Mediator note: Founders are either revered ancestors, living elders, or scandals. "Still around" can mean alive, dead but worshipped, exiled, or murdered. The answer hints at the Crawler's politics.*

### How many people live aboard, roughly?

> _

*Mediator note: A dozen? Fifty? Two hundred? Smaller Crawlers feel like a found family; larger ones feel like a town with factions. This sets the scale of every interpersonal hook later.*

### Who actually runs the Crawler, day to day?

> _

*Mediator note: The setting says decisions are made communally. In practice there's always someone — a council, a senior mechanic everyone defers to, a charismatic loudmouth, a quiet quartermaster who controls the food. Find that person. Name them.*

### What does the Crawler owe, and to whom?

> _

*Mediator note: Debt is fuel for plot. Maybe the Crawler owes a corpo for the fission core it's running. Maybe it owes a waster settlement that sheltered it during a radstorm. Maybe it owes another Union Crawler for parts that were never paid back. Get specific.*

### What's broken right now?

> _

*Mediator note: A Crawler is always wounded somewhere. A leaking coolant line, a dead sensor array, a missing leg actuator they've been improvising around for months. Something specific and physical. The first session can open with a player working on it.*

## 2. The Region

*Where in the wastes are we? Geography shapes every other answer — the Arcos that loom here, the raiders who hunt these roads, the creatures adapted to this particular dying place.*

### What does this region look like?

> _

*Mediator note: The setting offers radiation deserts, rolling mountains, scrap-filled battlefields, dense abandoned cityscapes. Pick a primary terrain, then complicate it. "Desert, but the dunes are made of crushed cars." "Mountains, but the snow is grey and burns skin."*

### What do the locals call this region?

> _

*Mediator note: Place-names tell history. The Glassed Flats. The Long Cough. The Rustlands. The Quiet. Names often refer to what happened here, what's wrong with it, or what people have to do to survive it.*

### What's the weather doing to people here?

> _

*Mediator note: Setting mentions radiation storms and blacked-out skies as normal as rain and traffic. Be specific to this region. Are there acid rains in spring? A six-month dust season? Auroras that scramble Mech sensors?*

### What's the most dangerous natural feature in the region, and why do salvagers risk it anyway?

> _

*Mediator note: The "why anyway" is the important half. The danger has to offer something — a shortcut, salvage, a sacred site, the only fresh water for a hundred kilometers. Without the lure, nobody goes there and the danger never enters play.*

## 3. The Arcologies

*Pick **one or two** Arcos near enough to matter. These are the corporate megasettlements whose influence reaches into the region — by hunger, by force, or by the long patience of debt.*

> **Tip for the Mediator:** Don't over-design here. One vivid detail per Arco beats a full profile. The table will return to whichever Arco grabs them; you can flesh that one out later.

### Arco One

#### What's the Arco's name and physical form?

> _

*Mediator note: Spires, pyramids, biodomes, submerged cities. Make it visible from far away — players will see it on the horizon and feel things.*

#### Which corpo runs it, and what do they make or trade?

> _

*Mediator note: A corpo is defined by its product as much as its name. AquaCor sells clean water. Verdantia makes synthetic protein. SpineTec ships replacement parts. The product hints at the Arco's vulnerabilities and what salvagers might steal.*

#### What does this corpo want from the region, right now?

> _

*Mediator note: This is the live question. They're searching for a lost asset. They're cracking down on strikers. They're surveying for a new mining operation. "What they want now" is the engine of all corpo encounters this campaign.*

#### What's the Crawler's current relationship to this Arco — ignored, hunted, trading, or something stranger?

> _

*Mediator note: "Stranger" includes things like: the Crawler has an asset hidden inside; a defector lives aboard; one of the players has family in the Arco. Push for nuance.*

## 4. The Raiders

*Pick **one or two** raider groups operating in the region. Raiders are wasters who chose the gun over the long slow death — sometimes from desperation, sometimes from ambition, sometimes from a hate the Crawler doesn't yet understand.*

### Raider Group One

#### What does this group call itself, and what's their reputation?

> _

*Mediator note: Names should communicate something — The Lead Wives, The Carrion Choir, Tornquist's Boys. Reputation can be earned ("they crucify deserters") or inherited ("they were a corpo merc company once, fell apart, still wear the colors").*

#### Who leads them, and what do they want to become?

> _

*Mediator note: Ambition matters. A raider chief who wants to be a corpo merc captain plays differently from one who wants to burn every Arco. Push past "they're bad people" — every leader has a vision of a better life for themselves.*

#### Where do they operate, and what's their preferred prey?

> _

*Mediator note: A canyon ambush gang plays different from a fast-moving Mech convoy hunter. "Preferred prey" tells you who suffers — wasters? Corpo supply runs? Other Crawlers? The answer tells the table whether the raiders are their problem or someone else's.*

#### What does this group know about the Crawler, and how do they feel about it?

> _

*Mediator note: Have the Crawler and the raiders ever crossed? If yes, who came out ahead? Even "they've never met" is useful — it means the first encounter is fresh and unread.*

## 5. The Wasters

*Settlements and notable figures of the wasteland — the static, vulnerable folk who can't pick up and run when corpos come knocking. These are the people the Crawler trades with, defends, sometimes fails, sometimes is failed by.*

### Settlement One

#### What's it called, and what does it look like?

> _

*Mediator note: A patched-together truck-stop town? A collective grown into the bones of a crashed transport? A handful of families inside a half-buried bunker? Make it physically specific.*

#### What does this settlement have that the Crawler needs?

> _

*Mediator note: Could be material (fresh water, machine parts, fuel), informational (a working radio post, an elder who remembers the old maps), or social (a relative of a Pilot lives here). The "need" creates reason to visit.*

#### What does the settlement need that the Crawler can provide?

> _

*Mediator note: Mech labor for a single hard job. Protection during a known raid season. Salvage from a place too dangerous for them. The mutual exchange is what keeps the relationship from being charity.*

#### Who in the settlement does the Crawler deal with by name?

> _

*Mediator note: One named person is worth more than a faceless village. Give them a quirk, a beef, a hope. They'll come up again.*

## 6. The Bio-Titans

*Behemoth monstrosities, part flesh, part machine. They may be local truth or distant rumor. Either way, the table needs to agree on what's whispered about them.*

### Is there a Bio-Titan rumored in this region?

> _

*Mediator note: "Rumored" is doing work. The answer can be: yes, definitely, we've seen it; yes, but only in stories; no, but everyone's nervous because of stories from further out. All three are good. Pick the flavor that fits the campaign's appetite for monsters.*

### What's the rumor say about it — form, behavior, where it's seen?

> _

*Mediator note: Push for sensory detail. "Long-legged, walks on water somehow, leaves a trail of dead birds." "Burrows. The ground hums for hours before it surfaces." "Hasn't been confirmed sighted in twenty years but the cattle still won't graze in the eastern flats."*

### Has anyone aboard the Crawler claimed to have seen it? Are they believed?

> _

*Mediator note: A discredited witness is a richer character than a believed one. Maybe a Pilot's grandparent swore they saw it as a child and the rest of the Crawler has rolled their eyes ever since. That's a thread.*

### What does the Crawler do when the Bio-Titan comes up at the dinner table?

> _

*Mediator note: This is a culture question. Are they brave about it? Superstitious? Do they have a song? A ritual? A rule about saying its name? The Crawler's emotional posture toward the titan tells you how the table will feel when one actually shows up.*

## 7. The Meld

*The intelligent nanite hive-mind, of the stars. They lurk at the edges of the world. Most are dust. Some are not. The table decides whether the Meld are here, or only a story told here.*

### Is the Meld present in this region, or only rumored from elsewhere?

> _

*Mediator note: Both are valid and lead to very different campaigns. "Present" makes the Meld a recurring threat or temptation (Active Meld is worth a Tech 6 Scrap each, per the setting). "Rumored only" makes the Meld an existential dread that *might* arrive — useful for slower-burn horror.*

### Who first whispered about the Meld here, and what did they say?

> _

*Mediator note: Origin of the rumor matters. A returning salvager who came back wrong. A corpo broadcast on a frequency that shouldn't have been broadcasting. A waster child who drew the same impossible spiral over and over before stopping. Make the first-contact memorable.*

### Does anyone in the region want the Meld — for use, for worship, for profit?

> _

*Mediator note: The setting flags that corpos exploit Meld, others worship them. Even in a "rumored only" campaign, there's usually one local who's *very* interested in the topic. That person is a future story.*

## 8. Hooks & Beefs

*Open questions for future sessions. Don't resolve them now — capture them, then leave them to ripen. These are the threads you'll pull on between sessions.*

### Who owes the Crawler something, and hasn't paid?

> _

*Mediator note: A bounty unpaid, a favor unreturned, salvage promised and never delivered. Specific names from earlier sections are best.*

### Who does the Crawler owe, and what happens when collection comes?

> _

*Mediator note: Mirror of the above. The collector arriving is a session opener.*

### What is the Crawler arguing about right now, internally?

> _

*Mediator note: Politics aboard. Should we trade with this Arco or boycott them? Should we let the wasters into the Crawler permanently? Is the captain past their prime? Internal conflict is fuel and the players are *in* it, not above it.*

### What's the one thing nobody on the Crawler wants to talk about?

> _

*Mediator note: The buried thing. The shameful trade, the lost crew member, the deal made with a corpo in a desperate winter. Don't push for full disclosure now — just get the shape of the silence. The campaign will excavate it.*
