# Pilots & Crew

A Mediator-facing worksheet for capturing the table's pilots — as people, not as stat blocks. Run alongside or just after the **Session Zero** worksheet; this one assumes a region, a Crawler, and at least a few NPCs already exist.

This sheet has two parts:

1. **Per-pilot blocks** — one for each pilot at the table, covering their Profile, Cabin, and Tie to the World. The template repeats these as needed.
2. **Table-level pages** — the Web of bonds between pilots, the Crawler Roles, and Between-Session Notes. These exist once for the whole table.

## How to Use This Worksheet

Same rhythm as Session Zero: you ask, players answer, you record. Mechanical choices (Pilot Class, equipment, abilities) come from the rulebook — this worksheet is for everything *around* those choices.

Plan on **15–20 minutes per pilot** for their block, then another 20–30 minutes together for the Web and the Crawler Roles pages. For a four-pilot table, that's roughly **two hours total.** Split across two short sessions if needed.

Each pilot's block covers three short sections: **Profile** (who they are), **Cabin** (where they live and what that says about them), and **Tie to the World** (a named NPC connection drawn from Session Zero). Then the table works together on **The Web** (how pilots connect to each other) and **The Crawler Roles** (what jobs hold the Crawler together).

If a player draws a blank on a question, skip it — half the fun of a TTRPG is discovering the answer in play. Mark blanks with a "?" so you remember to revisit.

## The Web — Pilot ↔ Pilot

*Now that every pilot is a person, the table builds the web between them. Don't pair everyone — sparse webs are more interesting than complete ones. Aim for **2–4 bonds total** across the table. The pilots without bonds aren't *unconnected* — they're connections waiting to form in play.*

> **Tip for the Mediator:** Let the table negotiate this together. "Did your pilot know mine before the Crawler?" is one of the best questions a TTRPG table ever asks itself.

## Crawler Roles

*Pilots don't just pilot. Aboard the Crawler, everyone has a function — sometimes formal, sometimes whatever-they-fell-into. This page makes the team's shape visible: who covers what, where the gaps are, who's overcommitted.*

> **Tip for the Mediator:** Go around the table and have each player declare their pilot's primary aboard-Crawler role first. Then look at the list together. The gaps are story. "We have no medic" is a setup for the session where someone gets hurt and the crew has to improvise. Don't fill the gaps now — leave them visible.

*Mediator note: Roles can be functional (mechanic, cook, scout, medic, quartermaster, comms, archivist), social (peacemaker, troublemaker, organizer, the person everyone goes to), or both. The "second thing they're known for" gives a pilot range — the mechanic who also brews terrible liquor, the scout who fixes everyone's dreams up into stories. Use one line per pilot; six lines covers a full 3–6 player table.*

### Looking at the list together: what role is *missing* from this crew?

> _

*Mediator note: This is the most generative question on the whole worksheet. Whatever the table answers — that's a hook for Session One or Two. They need to find a medic. They need to recruit a scout. They need to figure out who's going to handle comms when the corpos start jamming. Write it down and use it.*
